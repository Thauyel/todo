# Fudgy One-Bowl Brownies

Quick, dense, crackly-top brownies. One bowl, no mixer. ~25 min total.
Makes a 9x9" (23x23cm) pan, about 12 squares.

## Ingredients
- 200 g dark chocolate (70 %), chopped
- 180 g unsalted butter
- 3 large eggs, room temp
- 200 g granulated sugar
- 100 g all-purpose flour
- 30 g cocoa powder
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 1/2 tsp fine salt
- 100 g chocolate chips or chunks (optional, but worth it)

## Method
1. Preheat oven to 175 °C (350 °F). Line a 9x9" pan with parchment, leaving overhang on two sides.
2. Melt chocolate and butter together — microwave in 20-second bursts, stirring between each, or over a bain-marie. Let cool 5 min.
3. In the same bowl, whisk in sugar, then eggs one at a time, then vanilla. Whisk vigorously for ~30 s until glossy and a little ribbony.
4. Sift in flour, cocoa, and salt. Fold gently with a spatula until just combined — do not overmix.
5. Stir in chocolate chips. Pour into the pan, smooth the top.
6. Bake 22-25 min. The center should look *just* set with a tiny wobble; a toothpick should come out with moist crumbs, not wet batter.
7. Cool completely in the pan on a rack. Lift out by the parchment, slice into 12.

## Notes
- For extra fudginess, swap 30 g of the butter for 30 g of neutral oil.
- A pinch of espresso powder in the batter deepens the chocolate flavor without tasting like coffee.
- Best the day they're made. Store in an airtight tin up to 3 days.
